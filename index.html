🔴 Weird & Wonderful 19 CFR Regulations

🔪 The One-Armed Switchblade Exception

19 CFR § 12.98: Switchblade knives are generally prohibited... UNLESS you have only one arm!

If you have one arm, you can import a switchblade with a blade up to 3 inches long. The regulation specifically acknowledges the utility for individuals with limited manual dexterity. This is a real regulation! 🤯

💍 Married Women as Sureties (Historical)

Former 19 CFR § 113.35(b)(2): This regulation used to state that a married woman could act as surety on a customs bond... unless her state prohibited it.

This reflected outdated legal views on married women's contractual capacity. It's been removed to align with modern equal rights standards. But it was on the books for decades! 👰

🚫 Felons Need Permission

19 CFR § 111.53(e): Customs brokers cannot employ anyone convicted of a felony without written approval from CBP.

Even if you want to give someone a second chance, you need the Assistant Commissioner's blessing first.

📦 HTS Code Oddities

🐵 You Can Import Primates

HTS Code 0106.11: Live primates (monkeys, apes) have their own classification!

Yes, you can technically import live monkeys. Though good luck with the permits, CITES regulations, and explaining to your neighbors why there's a chimp in your backyard. 🙈

🐋 Whale Imports Are Classified

HTS Code 0106.12: Live whales, dolphins, and porpoises have their own code.

Because apparently someone thought "we should probably have a code for when someone tries to import a whale." International conservation laws make this nearly impossible, but the code exists! 🐳

🚽 Toilet Seats Are Plastic, Not Furniture

HTS Code 3924.90: Toilet seats are classified under plastics (Chapter 39), not furniture (Chapter 94).

This has caused actual trade disputes. Is it furniture you sit on, or a plastic household article? The HTS says: plastic! Classification matters for duty rates. 💺

🏠 Prefab Buildings Are Furniture

Chapter 94: Prefabricated buildings are classified as "furniture."

Your shed? Furniture. Your garage? Furniture. That modular home? Believe it or not... furniture! Chapter 94 is wild. 🏗️

🐶 Dogs Are "Other Mammals"

HTS Code 0106.19: Man's best friend is classified as "other live mammals not elsewhere specified."

Dogs, one of the most common domestic animals, get lumped into a catch-all "other" category. Meanwhile, primates and whales get their own specific codes. Priorities! 🐕

💡 Did You Know?

📏 The HTS Has 99 Chapters

From live animals (Chapter 1) to works of art (Chapter 97), the HTS classifies everything. Chapter 98 is for special classification provisions, and Chapter 99 is for temporary legislation.

⏱️ You Get 3.375 Minutes Per Question

With 80 questions in 4.5 hours (270 minutes), you have about 3 minutes and 22 seconds per question. Time management is CRITICAL!

📚 19 CFR Has 200+ Parts

Title 19 of the Code of Federal Regulations contains over 200 parts covering everything from broker regulations to drawback procedures. It's a LOT to know!

🎯 75% Is Passing

You need 60 out of 80 questions correct to pass. That means you can miss 20 questions and still become a licensed customs broker. But with those pass rates... good luck! 🍀

🌍 The HTS Is International

The Harmonized System (HS) is used by over 200 countries worldwide. The first 6 digits are standardized internationally, while countries add their own digits for more specificity.

💰 Classification Affects Duty Rates

Getting the HTS code wrong can mean paying the wrong duty rate - sometimes 0% vs 25%+! That's why classification is such a big part of the exam.

🎯 Top 10 Most Tested 19 CFR Parts

Focus your study on these sections - they appear most frequently on the exam!

Part 111 – Customs Brokers
HIGH PRIORITY

Licensing, responsibilities, ethical standards, and broker compliance requirements.

Key sections: §111.1 (definitions), §111.28 (responsible supervision), §111.29 (notification requirements), §111.53 (employment restrictions)
Part 141 – Entry of Merchandise
HIGH PRIORITY

Requirements and procedures for importing goods into the U.S.

Key sections: §141.0a (entry types), §141.4 (entry requirements), §141.61 (entry documentation), §141.86 (missing documents)
Part 152 – Classification & Appraisement
HIGH PRIORITY

How goods are classified and valued for duty purposes.

Key sections: §152.101-107 (valuation methods), §152.1 (definitions), transaction value rules
Part 142 – Entry Process
MEDIUM PRIORITY

Steps for entry and release of merchandise.

Key sections: §142.3 (entry procedures), §142.16 (entry summary documentation)
Part 113 – Customs Bonds
MEDIUM PRIORITY

Bonding requirements for customs activities.

Key sections: §113.62 (basic importation bond), §113.63 (continuous bond), bond amounts
Part 134 – Country of Origin Marking
MEDIUM PRIORITY

Marking requirements to indicate country of origin.

Key sections: §134.1 (definitions), §134.11 (general marking requirements), §134.35 (exceptions)
Part 163 – Recordkeeping
STANDARD

Maintenance of records for customs transactions.

Key sections: §163.1 (retention period - 5 years), §163.2 (what records to keep)
Part 133 – Intellectual Property
STANDARD

Protection of trademarks, trade names, and copyrights.

Key sections: §133.21 (recordation), §133.23 (restrictions on importation)
Part 171 – Fines, Penalties & Forfeitures
STANDARD

Penalties for violations and mitigation procedures.

Key sections: §171.1 (definitions), mitigation guidelines, penalty amounts
Part 174 – Protests
STANDARD

Process for challenging customs decisions.

Key sections: §174.12 (filing protests), §174.13 (time limits - 180 days)

💡 Proven Study Strategies

📑 Tab Your Books

Create a comprehensive tabbing system for 19 CFR and HTSUS. Color-code by topic. You'll save precious minutes during the exam!

🗂️ Use Flashcards

Memorize CFR part numbers, key definitions, and common classifications. Digital or physical - whatever works for you!

📝 Practice Past Exams

CBP publishes past exams with answer keys. Do them ALL under timed conditions. Analyze your mistakes!

⏱️ Master Time Management

3.375 minutes per question. If stuck, mark it and move on. Come back with fresh eyes later.

📚 Study the GRIs

The 6 General Rules of Interpretation are the legal framework for classification. Apply them sequentially!

👥 Join a Study Group

Discuss complex topics with others. Teaching someone else is the best way to learn!

📖 Read Explanatory Notes

HTS Explanatory Notes provide detailed guidance for classification and resolve ambiguities.

🔄 Review Regularly

Spaced repetition works! Review material multiple times over weeks/months, not just cramming.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Not Practicing Under Timed Conditions

The time pressure is REAL. Practice with a timer to build speed and stamina.

❌ Skipping the "Boring" Parts

Bonds, protests, and penalties might seem dry, but they're tested! Don't skip them.

❌ Relying Only on Study Guides

Study guides are helpful, but you MUST read the actual CFR and HTSUS. No shortcuts!

❌ Not Updating Your Materials

Regulations change! Make sure you're studying the current version of 19 CFR.

❌ Misreading Questions

Read carefully! Questions often have qualifiers like "EXCEPT" or "NOT" that change the answer.

❌ Poor Tab Organization

If you can't find information quickly, your tabs are useless. Practice using them!
